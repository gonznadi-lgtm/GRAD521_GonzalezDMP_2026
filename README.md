# GRAD521_GonzalezDMP_2026

For my research project, I am looking at the role of p38, a MAP kinase, in
Parkinson’s disease (PD). I am looking at its effect on environmentally induced and
familial forms of PD. My project focuses on three aims, the first of which is the impact of
p38 overexpression and knockout in environmentally induced PD. This aim is to
understand p38’s role in cellular processes, as previous work has shown that when p38
is knocked down, we observe PD-like symptoms, and when p38 is overexpressed, it’s
protective against PD-like phenotypes. Interestingly, in the disease state, p38 is
persistently activated, which in turn leads to disease progression. In my second aim, I
am examining the activation state of p38 and its role in disease progression. P38 is an
enzyme that undergoes dual phosphorylation. I am examining various Drosophila
constructs in which p38 exhibits different phosphorylation states and performing
experiments on those flies. My third aim is to look at p38’s role in protein autophagic
regulation in familial forms, as p38 has been shown to interact with parkin, a commonly
mutated gene in PD that is also involved in the autophagosome degradation pathway.
